Beocat Poem

Reflection

Beocat is a parody of the story Beowulf. It was filled with Alliteration and kenning, and was written in the same style as the original work Beowulf. The story of Beowulf is that of a monster named Grendel who is tormenting a group of people. Grendel then is eventually killed my the Hero Beowulf. Grendel has a dog, and the Parody is that Beowulf has a cat, Beocat and it wants a human to open the doors so it can go fight the dog. Some things I have learned from this story are thinking outside of the box, being more creative, making my things more interesting. Both the word choice and the Idea of the poem itself suggests that the Author was very good at thinking outside of the box. I feel like I could improve in this in my assignments. I’m not really the most creative person, so this is something I can definitely improve on weather it be in assignments, in my programming or in my spare time. This is probably the biggest effect Beocat has had on me. Another one is how informal and fun this piece of literature is. I also need to work on this. In school it seems that everything has to be serious all of the time. It is fun to have some fun sometimes.

Analysis:

The author of this poem had a lot of alliteration. One example of alliteration was “The wary war-cat, whiskered paw-wielder.” This is really good alliteration. It shows that the author didn’t really want this to be necessarily historically accurate, along with kenning in this poem. But this poem is really laid back, but It has some interesting similarities to Beowulf. Another example is how the Author chooses to start the poem. “Brave Beocat, Brood-kit of Ecgthmeow…” I think the author chose to do this because it puts more emphasis into all the words that start with ‘b’. The author chooses to do this on almost every line, which makes the poem much more interesting than what it would be without. This is also part of the reason the Author put in so much kenning. Sometimes, you need some more words that start with the same letter, so you have to be creative. This is very well demonstrated in the following line. “Bearer of the burnished neck-belt, gold braided collar-band”. As you can see, neck-belt isn’t actually a thing, but you can tell it is a collar. These are the two literary elements that the author used really well in this poem to enrich the meaning of it.

Analysis:

One really prominent element in the epic of gilgamesh is foreshadowing. One spot where this shows a lot is when Enkidu and Gilgamesh have to fight a demon in the cedar forest. Enkidu ends up having three dreams and they try to interpret them as to whether they will have good luck or bad luck. One of his dreams was that they were walking through a deep gorge and a mountain fell on them. Enkidu interprets his dream and says they don’t have to worry about anything. He interprets it on how the demon is the mountain and they will die. But they decide it might not be real. After another series of dreams they end up fighting the demon Humbaba. They are not doing very well, and Gilgamesh and Enkidu keeps trying to encourage each other, eventually Gilgamesh pleads to one of the gods and the god send lots of storms which helps him defeat Humbaba. This literary element is used to explain how hard the fight against this demon is. Even though he ended up winning the gods cursed enkidu and made him die. Which is also foreshadowing that he will someday die. The rest of the book is about how Gilgamesh is trying to overcome death which he is destined that he will face.
